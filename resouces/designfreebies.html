Color Theory

The 60-30-10 Rule

This classic decorating rule is a timeless design principle that helps create balanced color schemes:

  • 60% of a dominant color
  • 30% of a secondary color
  • 10% of an accent color

Typography

Font Pairing Principles

Effective typography combines fonts that complement each other while creating visual hierarchy:

  1. Pair a serif with a sans-serif
  2. Limit to 2-3 typefaces per project
  3. Ensure sufficient contrast between heading and body fonts

Professional Font Combinations

Playfair Display

+ Open Sans - Elegant and readable combination for editorial designs

Montserrat

+ Lora - Modern yet classic pairing for websites

Typography Pro Tip:

For optimal readability, maintain line height between 1.4-1.6× your font size and limit line length to 50-75 characters.

Composition & Layout

The Rule of Thirds

Divide your canvas into a 3×3 grid and place key elements along these lines or their intersections for more dynamic compositions.

Advanced Tip: Combine with the Golden Ratio (1:1.618) for naturally pleasing proportions.

Visual Hierarchy

Guide the viewer's eye through deliberate sizing, spacing and placement:

  • Size: Larger elements get more attention
  • Color: High contrast stands out
  • Whitespace: Creates focus and breathing room
